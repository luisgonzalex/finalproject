Health Spending in El Salvador

From the 1703 surveyed households in El Salvador, had some sort of health expense within the six months previous to being surveyed. In total, this was about $285,513 USD, or about $167 per household (1465.80 Salvadorian Colons).

Impact of Remittances

618 households (about 36% of the total surveyed population) remarked that they receive remittances, or financial aid from friends or family members form abroad. From among all of the households the total amount of remittances received is $70,745.25 USD. A third of those households who receive remittances spend about 33% of it on health-related expenses on average. This is a total of $23,628.91 USD spent on healthcare.

Concerns About Health

Of a sample of 317 that remarked that health was one of their biggest concerns. 61% of the respondents who have concerns about healthcare do not receive remittances. However, households that received remittances comprised only a 39% of those worried about healthcare.
