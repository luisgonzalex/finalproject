How much is spent on healthcare?

From the 1703 surveyed households in El Salvador, had some sort of health expense within the six months previous to being surveyed. In total, this was about $285,513 USD, or about $167 per household (1465.80 Salvadorian Colons).

How do remittances help?

618 households (about 36% of the total surveyed population) remarked that they receive remittances, or financial aid from friends or family members form abroad.

From among all of the households the total amount of remittances received is $70,745.25 USD. A third of those households who receive remittances spend about 33% of it on health-related expenses on average. This is a total of $23,628.91 USD spent on healthcare.

Is health a concern?

Of a sample of 317 that remarked that health was one of their biggest concerns. 61% of the respondents who have concerns about healthcare do not receive remittances. However, households that received remittances comprised only a 39% of those worried about healthcare. Even though, remittances don't fully dispel the concern about healthcare, they may still help lower the anxieties about it.

Who gets help?

Even though healthcare is a big expenditure for many of those who were surveyed, only 29 households receive any form of healthcare aid. 22 of them receive government health aid and 7 obtain help from churches, NGOs, private companies, or the United Nations. The proportion of households who receive health assistance is lower than for those who receive no assistance. However, assistane is still scarce throughout the entire population.
